Please make sure you read and understand these instructions carefully. Please keep this document open while reviewing, and refer to it as needed.


Evaluation Criteria:

Voice (1-3): Measure how uniquely and recognizably the author expresses themselves through their choice of words, sentence structure, and stylistic tools.

- 1: Poor. The writing lacks a distinct voice, making it difficult to distinguish from others or fully convey the author's unique perspective or attitude.
- 2: Fair. The writing shows signs of a unique voice through occasional use of distinctive word choices or sentence structures, but it is not consistently recognizable.
- 3: Good. The writing clearly demonstrates a unique and recognizable voice, distinguished by purposeful and consistent use of stylistic tools, word choices, and sentence structures.
