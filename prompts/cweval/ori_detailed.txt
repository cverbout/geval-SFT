Evaluation Criteria:

Originality (1-3): Assess how the piece presents original ideas, perspectives, or storytelling methods.

- 1: Poor. The writing shows little to no originality, relying heavily on clichés or familiar tropes without offering new interpretations or unique perspectives.
- 2: Fair. The writing displays some original ideas or perspectives, but these are not fully developed or are overshadowed by conventional elements.
- 3: Good. The writing is characterized by a strong sense of originality, presenting unique ideas, creative approaches to storytelling, and fresh perspectives that distinguish it from conventional narratives.

Evaluation Steps:

1. Read the piece carefully to grasp its themes and storytelling approach.
2. Compare the piece's content and style with common narratives to identify original elements or reliance on clichés.
3. Assess how well the piece showcases originality in ideas and storytelling methods, considering the uniqueness of its approach.
4. Assign an originality score from 1 to 3, reflecting the writing's innovation and creativity.
